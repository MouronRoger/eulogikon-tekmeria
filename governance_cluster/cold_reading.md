## The engine

One epistemic move: **blindness plus mechanical scope.**

A reader with no briefing on the intended structure follows what the thing
actually does. A mechanical census fixes what exists before anyone interprets
what it means. Scope without blindness only confirms the planner's blind spots.
Blindness without scope is an entry-point lottery. Together they approximate
what is really there, seen without intended-structure bias, which neither lens
yields alone.

## The blind-integrity protocol

Non-negotiable.

1. **Mechanical scope floor first, by you, the coordinator.** Census what
   exists: the rows, the files, the claims. This answers what exists, not what
   it means.
2. **One question per surface, to independent readers.** No briefing, no
   doctrine, **no file list** (handing the reader your census turns discovery
   into verification and the rule is violated), no prior-session context, no
   rubric. Every claim cites something actually read.
3. **The tracer and the judge are different passes.** Readers explore and
   record what they find. A separate assessment pass, yours, classifies. Never
   fuse them.
4. **Absence of a current violator is not proof of safety.** "Nothing does that
   today" is a fact about today, not about the boundary.
5. **Verify the instrument's claims yourself.** A cold reader is an instrument,
   not gospel. Before surfacing any finding as a defect, re-read the thing it
   cites.

## Applied to a finished Tekmerion

This is the Phase 7 pass, and it is the one that catches what the verification
report structurally cannot: the report checks that every claim is backed, and
cannot check whether the page reads as evidence to someone who was not there
when it was assembled.

The reader gets **the page and nothing else**. No store, no ledger, no
verification report, no `STYLE.md`, no knowledge of the hinge. It answers:

- What does this piece claim?
- What does each claim rest on, and is that on the page?
- Does any sentence require information not present?
- Where does it tell rather than show?

Then you assess. A finding is fixed and the affected verification sections are
regenerated.

## Applied to the repo

Same protocol, subject changed. What actually writes the store. What actually
reads it. What actually produces a published page. Where a rule is really
stated, as against where it claims to be stated.

Dispositions, one per finding: **collapse** (one owner absorbs it),
**integrate** (it belongs with a concern that already exists), **track** (a
named alarm in `work_reports/`, not silent deferral), or **load-bearing** (it
is correct as it stands, and the reason is recorded).

## Prohibited defenses

Sanctioning is a rebuttable presumption, not an exemption. Nothing is immune.

"It is the idiom", "it is how the other essays do it", "it has always been this
way", and "it is sanctioned" are **prohibited defenses**. Mark the presumption,
then make it defend itself.

## Reject list

- A read that patches during discovery. Discovery informs; it does not fix.
- Handing the reader the store, the report, or the style guide.
- Being both tracer and judge.
- Ending in a backlog note rather than a disposition.
- Treating a cold reader's finding as a defect without re-reading the line it
  cites.
