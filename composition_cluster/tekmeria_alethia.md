Τεκμήριον · Ἀλήθεια
Etymology and Conceptual Field
The Greek term ἀλήθεια derives from the privative ἀ- + λήθη/lēthē ("forgetfulness, concealment"), meaning literally "un-concealment" or "disclosure." This etymological resonance—truth as that which emerges from hiddenness—permeates its philosophical career. The alpha-privative marks a removal of concealment rather than a positive possession; ἀλήθεια is what becomes manifest when λήθη is stripped away. The verb ἀληθεύω/alētheuō ("to speak truth, to be true") and the adjective ἀληθής/alēthēs ("true, real, genuine") share this root, grounding the concept in a kinetic process of revealing rather than static correspondence.

Archaic and Pre-Socratic Attestations
Aesop: ἀλήθεια as Practical Power
In Aesop's fables, ἀλήθεια appears as a practical force with tangible consequences:

πολλάκις ἀλήθεια καὶ παρὰ πολεμίοις ἰσχύει pollakis alētheia kai para polemiois ischuei Often ἀλήθεια has power even among enemies

—Aesop, Fables (Eulogikon: aqo-ap#837887), 164 1

Here ἀλήθεια is not abstract correctness but a capacity (ἰσχύει/ischuei) that operates across hostile boundaries. The kinetic substrate is evident: ἀλήθεια as active efficacy.

Theano of Croton: Pythagorean Soteriology
The Pythagorean tradition personifies ἀλήθεια as divine witness:

θεὸς ὁρᾷ καὶ δικάζει μάλιστα ἡ ἀλήθεια theos hora kai dikazei malista hē alētheia God sees and judges; ἀλήθεια most of all

—Theano of Croton, Pythagorean Teachings (Eulogikon: bhc-aa#744896), 200

The superlative μάλιστα/malista positions ἀλήθεια above even divine perception as the ultimate criterion. ἀλήθεια is not merely revealed but itself reveals and judges.

[Phalaris]: Epistolary Personification
ἡ ἀλήθεια προσβεβαιώσῃ hē alētheia prosbebaiōsē May ἀλήθεια confirm [it]

—[Phalaris], Ethical Letters (Eulogikon: auk-aa#1679534), 23 2

Source status: The letters attributed to Phalaris of Acragas are a known literary forgery (likely 2nd c. CE). The Greek is transmitted as pseudonymous epistolography. The personification of ἀλήθεια as confirming witness continues the Pythagorean pattern.

[Heraclitus]: Sibylline ἀλήθεια
ἀλήθεια ἔβλεπε alētheia eblepe ἀλήθεια saw

—[Heraclitus], Letters to Heraclitus (Eulogikon: bju-aa#344471), 8 2

Source status: This is from the spurious epistolary tradition attributed to Heraclitus, not the philosopher's authentic fragments. The personification (ἀλήθεια as subject of ὁράω/horaō) is consistent with the archaic pattern but cannot be attributed to the historical Heraclitus with confidence.

Philolaus: ἀλήθεια as Connate to Number
Philolaus offers a decisive ontological shift:

ἀλήθεια οἰκεῖον καὶ σύμφυτον τᾶι τῶ ἀριθμῶ γενεᾶι alētheia oikeion kai sumphuton tai tō arithmō geneai ἀλήθεια is connate and co-natural to the race of number

—Philolaus, On the Nature of the Cosmos (Eulogikon: bue-ab#1679737), 11

The dative τᾶι... γενεᾶι/tai... geneai with οἰκεῖον/oikeion and σύμφυτον/sumphuton embeds ἀλήθεια in the Pythagorean number-ontology. ἀλήθεια is not imposed from without but grows together with (σύμφυτον/sumphuton) numerical reality. This is a kinetic substrate: ἀλήθεια as emergent from the relational structure of number.

[Hippocrates]: ἀλήθεια as Medical Witness
Ἀλήθεια... τοῦ ὑγιαίνειν παρὰ Δημοκρίτεω μένει Alētheia... tou hygiainein para Dēmokriteō menei ἀλήθεια... of health remains with Democritus

—[Hippocrates], Letters (Eulogikon: bww-ae#2122158), 15

Source status: The Hippocratic letters are part of the wider Hippocratic corpus but are likely pseudonymous. The personification of ἀλήθεια as remaining/lasting (μένει/menei) with the physician continues the archaic pattern of ἀλήθεια as enduring witness.

Classical Period: Forensic, Rhetorical, and Philosophical Articulations
Gorgias: ἀλήθεια as Virtue of λόγος
λόγωι δὲ ἀλήθεια logōi de alētheia but ἀλήθεια by λόγος

—Gorgias, Helen Encomium (Eulogikon: eme-ab#1687307), 11 (tit)

The dative λόγωι/logōi is instrumental: ἀλήθεια is what λόγος accomplishes. This is not correspondence but performative disclosure—the power of speech to reveal.

Antiphon: ἀλήθεια as Forensic Ally
σύμμαχος ἦν ἡ ἀλήθεια summachos ēn hē alētheia ἀλήθεια was an ally

—Antiphon, On the Murder of Herodes (Eulogikon: bqs-ab#117851), Caed Her 37

The military metaphor (σύμμαχος/summachos) is striking: ἀλήθεια fights alongside the speaker. This is ἀλήθεια as kinetic force in the agonistic context of the lawcourt.

Thucydides: ἀλήθεια of Deeds vs. Boast
ἔργων ἐστὶν ἀλήθεια ergōn estin alētheia ἀλήθεια belongs to deeds

—Thucydides, Histories (Eulogikon: dny-aa#1253650), 2.41

Pericles' funeral oration opposes ἀλήθεια to λόγος in a specific sense: deeds are self-authenticating where words may deceive. The genitive ἔργων/ergōn is possessive or defining—ἀλήθεια is what deeds have, not what words claim.

Scholia on Euripides: ἀλήθεια as Self-Evidencing
οὐ ποικίλης δεῖται ἔξωθεν ἀπολογίας ἡ ἀλήθεια ou poikilēs deitai exōthen apologias hē alētheia ἀλήθεια does not need elaborate external defense

—Scholia on Euripides, Phoenissae (Eulogikon: cto-aj#1680676), sch Ph 454

This scholion captures the historiographical topos of ἀλήθεια as self-sufficient, requiring no rhetorical artifice.

Plato: The Ontological Turn
Republic: ἀλήθεια as Object of Philosophical ἔρως
ἀλήθεια, ἣν διώκειν αὐτὸν πάντως καὶ πάντῃ ἔδει alētheia, hēn diōkein auton pantōs kai pantē edei ἀλήθεια, which he must pursue in every way and everywhere

—Plato, Republic (Eulogikon: ffk-ag#1692586), Resp 490a

The relative clause with διώκειν/diōkein makes ἀλήθεια the hunted object of philosophical ἔρως. The pursuit is kinetic and totalizing (πάντως καὶ πάντῃ/pantōs kai pantē).

Timaeus: ἀλήθεια and Cosmic Proportion
ὅτιπερ πρὸς γένεσιν οὐσία, τοῦτο πρὸς πίστιν ἀλήθεια hotiper pros genesis ousia, touto pros pistin alētheia as οὐσία is to γένεσις, so ἀλήθεια is to πίστις

—Plato, Timaeus (Eulogikon: ffk-bf#1185998), Tim. 29d

The proportional analogy (ὅτιπερ... τοῦτο/hotiper... touto) maps the ontological hierarchy onto the epistemic. ἀλήθεια is correlated with οὐσία (standing reality), πίστις with γένεσις (coming-to-be). This is not correspondence but ontological proportion.

Parmenides: ἀλήθεια as What Escapes
σὲ διαφεύξεται ἡ ἀλήθεια se diapheuxetai hē alētheia ἀλήθεια will escape you

—Plato, Parmenides (Eulogikon: ffk-ak#1691515), Parm. 137c

The future διαφεύξεται/diapheuxetai with σέ/se as object personifies ἀλήθεια as actively eluding the inquirer who approaches incorrectly. ἀλήθεια is not passive but fugitive.

Aristotle: The Practical and Theoretical Syntheses
Nicomachean Ethics: ἀλήθεια as Activity of νοῦς
ἡ ἀλήθεια πρακτική... ὁμολόγως ἔχουσα τῇ ὀρέξει τῇ ὀρθῇ hē alētheia praktikē... homologōs echousa tē orexei tē orthē practical ἀλήθεια... having agreement with right ὀρέξις

—Aristotle, Nicomachean Ethics (Eulogikon: hgw-aw#1686195), EN 1139a

Aristotle's praktikē alētheia is not a state but an activity (energeia) of nous in agreement with orexis. The participle homologōs echousa marks a kinetic relation—ἀλήθεια as dynamic attunement between cognitive and desiderative powers.

Metaphysics: ἀλήθεια as τέλος of Theoretical Inquiry
θεωρητικῆς μὲν γὰρ τέλος ἀλήθεια theōrētikēs men gar telos alētheia for the τέλος of theoretical [inquiry] is ἀλήθεια

—Aristotle, Metaphysics (Eulogikon: hgw-bj#897699), Metaph. 993b

The genitive theōrētikēs (sc. epistēmēs) with telos makes ἀλήθεια the completion-point of inquiry. This is τέλος as the natural completion of an activity, not a designed purpose imposed from without.

On Virtues and Vices: ἀλήθεια as Consequent on Justice
ἀκολουθεῖ δὲ τῇ δικαιοσύνῃ... ἀλήθεια akolouthei de tē dikaiosynē... alētheia ἀλήθεια follows upon justice

—Aristotle, On Virtues and Vices (Eulogikon: hgw-at#199970), VV 1250b

The verb akolouthei marks a consequential relation: ἀλήθεια emerges as a necessary accompaniment of just action. This is ἀλήθεια as kinetic consequence, not static property.

Hellenistic Period: Stoic and Epicurean Materializations
Chrysippus: The Corporeal ἀλήθεια
ἡ μὲν ἀλήθεια σῶμά ἐστι, τὸ δὲ ἀληθὲς ἀσώματον hē men alētheia sōma esti, to de alēthes asōmaton ἀλήθεια is a body, but the ἀληθές is incorporeal

—Chrysippus, Fragments Logical and Physical (Eulogikon: kms-ac#2119648), 132

Chrysippus' distinction between alētheia (the body) and alēthes (the incorporeal) is foundational Stoic metaphysics. Alētheia as sōma is the corporeal state of the hēgemonikon when it grasps a lekton. The kinetic substrate is explicit: alētheia is a physical condition of the psychic pneuma, not an abstract relation.

Polystratus: ἀλήθεια as Lived Reality
ἡ ἐν τοῖς οὖσιν ἀλήθεια καὶ ὁ βίος αὐτὸς χρῆται hē en tois ousin alētheia kai ho bios autos chrētai the ἀλήθεια in things, which life itself uses

—Polystratus, On the Contempt of the Irrational (Eulogikon: jss-aa#1210351), col8b

The phrase hē en tois ousin alētheia locates ἀλήθεια immanently in beings; chrētai makes it an instrument of life itself. This is Epicurean or Peripatetic naturalism: ἀλήθεια as what living beings practically engage with.

Philo: ἀλήθεια as Self-Sufficient Praise
ἡ γὰρ ἀλήθεια αὐταρκέστατος ἔπαινος hē gar alētheia autarkestatos epainos for ἀλήθεια is the most self-sufficient praise

—Philo, Against Flaccus (Eulogikon: lgi-bf#610775), Flac 98

The superlative autarkestatos marks ἀλήθεια as requiring no external validation. This is the Hellenistic topos of self-evidencing ἀλήθεια, now applied to Jewish-apologetic context.

Roman Imperial: Stoic Ethics and Hermetic Ontology
Epictetus: ἀλήθεια as Locus of Good and Evil
ποῦ φύσις κακῶν καὶ ἀγαθῶν; οὗ καὶ ἀλήθεια pou physis kakōn kai agathōn; hou kai alētheia Where is the φύσις of evils and goods? Where ἀλήθεια is also

—Epictetus, Discourses (Eulogikon: ojw-ac#1974167), 2.2

The interrogative pou with the demonstrative hou locates ἀλήθεια as the place (topos) of ethical reality. This is Stoic immanence: ἀλήθεια is not transcendent but situated where physis operates.

Hermetica: ἀλήθεια and Mimetic Appearance
τὰ ἐπὶ γῆς ἀλήθεια μὲν οὐκ ἔστι, τῆς δὲ ἀληθείας μιμήματα ta epi gēs alētheia men ouk esti, tēs de alētheias mimēmata things on earth are not ἀλήθεια, but imitations of ἀλήθεια

—Hermetica, Stobaeus Hermetica (Eulogikon: oqk-ar#991495), 2A 2

The ouk esti / mimēmata distinction is Platonizing but with a Gnostic twist: earthly reality is not false but mimetic, derivative of a higher ἀλήθεια. The kinetic substrate is mimetic participation rather than static copy.

Nicomachus: Mathematical ἀλήθεια and Cosmic Vision
ἡ περὶ τοῦ παντὸς ἀλήθεια ὁρᾶται hē peri tou pantos alētheia horatai the ἀλήθεια concerning the all is seen

—Nicomachus, Arithmetic Introduction (Eulogikon: owy-aa#2151098), 1.3.6

The passive horatai with ἀλήθεια as subject continues the visual metaphor: ἀλήθεια is what becomes visible to mathematical inquiry. This is Pythagorean-Platonic synthesis.
