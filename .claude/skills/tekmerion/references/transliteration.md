# Transliteration: ALA-LC as practised in the Tekmeria

Standard American (ALA-LC) romanization, with diacritics carried over so the
reader can hear the accentuation. Derived from the published pieces; where the
published practice varies, the rule here follows the most recent piece.

## Vowels

- α a, ε e, η ē, ι i, ο o, ω ō
- υ standing alone: **y** (physis, psychē). υ in a diphthong: **u**
  (au, eu, ēu, ou, ui): toû, autoîs, eulógiston.

## Breathings and accents

- Rough breathing: **h** before the vowel (hē, hḗ, hoútō); on ρ: rh.
- Smooth breathing: nothing.
- Accents are kept on the Latin vowels: acute (katá -> katà when grave in
  context, phýsin, stérxis), circumflex (toû, pneûma, tê), grave as printed.
  Combine with macron where both apply: hḗ (ἥ), ṑ (ὢ).
- Elision apostrophe kept as printed: t᾽, d᾽.

## Iota subscript

Render the vowel with its accent, then **(i)** in parentheses:

- τῷ -> tô(i)
- τῇ -> tê(i)
- φιλοστοργίᾳ -> philostorgía(i)

(An older published piece renders these as ōi/ēi with combining diacritics,
e.g. thermō̂i. The parenthesis form is the current practice; do not mix the
two inside one piece.)

## Scope

- Transliterate the whole excerpt exactly as the Greek line runs, including
  particles and editorial brackets' contents (drop the brackets themselves in
  the transliteration line only if they would confuse the ear; keep them in
  the Greek line always).
- The transliteration line is italic, wrapped in the site's
  `p.translit` (see witness-block.md).
- In running English prose, use the same romanization for Greek words:
  pneûma, psychē, philostorgía, kólasis. Greek script in prose only where
  exact form matters (`STYLE.md` § Formatting cross-check).
